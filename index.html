Sample

From Chapter 05 — Quality

You can't inspect 100% of a run by hand, and you don't need to. The industry standard is AQL — Acceptable Quality Limit — a sampling method that tells you how many units to pull and how many defects allow you to accept or reject the batch.

Even at your scale, sort every fault into critical / major / minor and hold the factory to a written AQL standard in your PO. It converts "I'm not happy with these" into "this batch fails the AQL 2.5 major standard we agreed" — a conversation you can actually win. And inspect before you release the balance payment. Once the money and the goods have both left, "please fix these" becomes "please, if you feel like it."
